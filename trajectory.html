Meridian trajectory preview

How likely the events are, when they tend to happen, and what they cost in functional capacity.

This is a communication prototype. The Events view shows when each endpoint tends to happen and how often. The Functional capacity view shows the capacity curve that follows from those events plus functional reserve. Every number on the page is a synthetic placeholder. Nothing here is calibrated to a specific cohort or yet a validated Meridian model output.

What this page is testing

Event timing alone is incomplete. A useful forecast also needs a denominator: of comparable people, what share actually reach the endpoint by the modeled horizon? The Events view combines those two into one shape per endpoint. The Functional capacity view then shows the capacity curve that follows under a chosen level of functional reserve. Both views share an age axis and a single scenario controlled by the sliders below.

Method, in plain English

For each endpoint the prototype stores median timing, the central 80% timing window, and cumulative incidence by age 95. These numbers are not derived from ATM status, dyslipidemia, sex, or any age-specific cohort; they are placeholders chosen to test the visual grammar. Endpoints are marginal: not mutually exclusive, not competing-risk adjusted, and not a multi-state sequence. The horizon is age 95, the comparison is one synthetic risk state against another, and prior events, death, and endpoint dependencies are not modeled.

From risk to events to functional capacity

Two views of the same age axis under the same scenario. The Events view shows when each endpoint tends to occur and how often. The Functional capacity view shows the capacity curve that follows from those events plus functional reserve. Toggle between them. Disease risk moves the events. Functional reserve changes how steeply the capacity curve falls and how hard each event hits it.

The functional capacity scale runs 0 to 100, banded into five practical zones: 90 and above is full capacity with reserve, 75 to 90 is independent but narrowing, 60 to 75 is vulnerable independence, 45 to 60 is the independence-threshold zone, and below 45 is supported or dependent living.

Endpoint definitions

Definitions are visible because reviewers should be able to challenge the object being modeled. MACE and cancer have cleaner literature definitions. Mobility, independence, and MCI or dementia need stricter operational rules before clinical use.

Limitations

The displayed numbers are synthetic, not calibrated to any phenotype, and not the output of a fitted Meridian model. The lower-risk state is a synthetic comparison, not an attributable Meridian intervention effect. Death, prior events, and cross-endpoint dependencies are not modeled, so cumulative incidences are marginal and not a coherent multi-state sequence. Mobility, independence, and cognition still need stricter operational thresholds before any clinical use.

Calculation notes for reviewers who want the mechanics

For each endpoint e, the prototype stores: medianAgeHigh, p10AgeHigh, p90AgeHigh, incidenceHighBy95, maxMedianShift, incidenceLowBy95.
The displayed base is the central 80% conditional timing interval: p10 to p90 among synthetic cohort members who experience the endpoint by age 95.
Disease-risk scale r ranges from 0 to 1. At r = 0, selected event layer equals the higher-risk profile. At r = 1, selected event layer equals the lower-risk profile.
Functional-reserve scale q ranges from 0 to 1. It changes the functional capacity curve only. It does not change endpoint incidence, timing, or triangle geometry.
Functional capacity bands: 90 to 100 = full capacity with reserve; 75 to 90 = independent with narrowing reserve; 60 to 75 = vulnerable independence; 45 to 60 = independence threshold; below 45 = supported or dependent living.
selectedMedianAge = medianAgeHigh + maxMedianShift x r.
selectedIncidenceBy95 = incidenceHighBy95 minus ((incidenceHighBy95 minus incidenceLowBy95) x r).
selectedP10Age = p10AgeHigh + maxMedianShift x r. selectedP90Age = p90AgeHigh + maxMedianShift x r, with optional widening only if the calibrated model shows wider late-age uncertainty.
Functional capacity prototype: capacity(age) = baseline reserve minus age-related decline minus late-life terminal compression minus the sum of event impact weights multiplied by each endpoint's cumulative incidence and smoothed timing transition.
Disease risk affects the event terms: incidence, median timing, and when event burden begins. Functional reserve affects the consequence terms: starting reserve, decline slope, terminal compression, and event-impact multiplier. The two controls are intentionally orthogonal in this prototype.
