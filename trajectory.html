Meridian trajectory preview

Showing incidence and timing without implying patient-specific certainty.

This standalone page is meant for reviewer feedback. It tests a communication layer: every endpoint needs both a denominator answer and a timing answer. The values below are intentionally labeled as prototype values until they are replaced by model output.

Clinical status

Illustrative prototype. Not a patient-specific forecast. Not yet a validated Meridian risk model output.

What reviewers should judge

Does this make the distinction between cumulative event incidence and conditional event timing clear enough for physicians and patients?

What reviewers should not infer yet

The numeric deltas are not being claimed as intervention-attributable. They are placeholders for a higher-risk versus lower-risk profile comparison.

Trajectory concept
Reference cohort: ATM positive, dyslipidemic males age 45 to 50. Horizon: cumulative incidence by age 95. The chart shows marginal endpoint estimates. Events are not mutually exclusive and are not modeled here as a single sequence.

Defensibility fixes in this version
  • Numbers are labeled as prototype values.The page no longer implies these are final Meridian model outputs.
  • Horizon is explicit.Incidence means cumulative incidence by age 95, not vague lifetime risk.
  • Risk-state comparison is separated from plan effect.The lower-risk state is not presented as a proven causal result of Meridian interventions.
  • Event dependence is disclosed.These are marginal endpoints. They are not mutually exclusive and not a multi-state sequence model.
  • Endpoint definitions are visible.Reviewers can challenge what each event actually means instead of guessing.
What still needs real model work
  • Replace placeholder incidence.Each endpoint needs a source cohort, calibration method, and confidence interval.
  • Add competing-risk treatment.Death and prior events should be handled explicitly before this becomes clinical truth.
  • Define intervention attribution.If Meridian claims movement from high to low risk, the causal pathway and evidence must be specified.
  • Quantify uncertainty.The chart needs confidence bands or credible intervals once the model is real.
  • Validate functional endpoints.Mobility and independence are harder than MACE or cancer and need stricter definitions.
